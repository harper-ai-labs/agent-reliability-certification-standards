## Overview

The Agent Reliability Certification Standards (ARCS) define open, platform-agnostic criteria for certifying the reliability, security, and operational readiness of autonomous AI agents.

With 70–85% of AI projects failing to meet objectives and 95% of enterprise generative AI pilots falling short, the industry lacks a shared standard for what "production-ready" means for autonomous agents. ARCS fills that gap.

## Core Testing Domains

1. **Memory & Context Management** — persistence, recall, overflow handling
2. **Tool Use & Integration** — reliability, error recovery, rate limiting
3. **Autonomy & Decision-Making** — ambiguity handling, risk detection, escalation
4. **Security & Access Control** — authentication, prompt injection, data isolation
5. **Performance & Scalability** — latency, throughput, resource management
6. **Human Oversight & Control** — escalation paths, audit logging, emergency stops
7. **Cross-Session Continuity** — state restoration, graceful degradation
8. **Compliance & Ethics** — data handling, bias detection, regulatory alignment
